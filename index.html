How to make the best cappuccino

By Ori Dvori ☕

Hello and welcome to my site!

My name is Ori Dvori, and I’m excited to show you how to make an authentic Italian-style cappuccino. Serve it with biscuits, relax, and enjoy the moment. ☕ 😍

My story

I started making high-quality cappuccino about three years ago. One day at work, I tried using an espresso machine—but the result was terrible. The coffee tasted sour, looked too dark, and even sugar couldn’t fix it.

That failure sparked my curiosity. I began imagining the perfect cup: rich espresso combined with smooth, velvety milk. I wanted to create something people would truly enjoy—and remember.

I started learning, practicing, and improving step by step. I even took a barista course where I learned professional techniques and consistency. Today, I love making cappuccino for friends and family and seeing their reactions.

On this site, I’ll share everything I’ve learned so you can also make an excellent cappuccino at home.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to make a cappuccino so good people will ask for another cup.

  1. Grind the Coffee Beans

    Use high-quality coffee beans and grind them finely. For a single shot, measure about 9 grams. Avoid grinding too fine to prevent bitterness.

  2. Break Up Clumps

    Use a needle tool to break up clumps for even extraction.

  3. Level the Grounds

    Use a leveler to distribute the coffee evenly in the portafilter.

  4. Tamp Evenly

    Press the coffee firmly (around 30 lbs of pressure) to ensure even water flow.

  5. Add a Puck Screen

    Place a puck screen on top to improve water distribution.

  6. Steam the Milk

    Steam cold milk until it becomes smooth and velvety. Keep the wand just below the surface and create a whirlpool effect.

  7. Pour and Serve

    Pour the milk into the espresso and create your desired pattern. Optionally, add cocoa or cinnamon.
